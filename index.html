As far as I remember, I started poking around HTML when I was in 7th grade.
I wanted to make my own web pages so I was snooping around Google to see how that was possible.

The first few lines of HTML that I wrote were most probably a link with a a:hover effect, When I opened my page on a browser and the mouseover effect worked, I remember being excited like the little kid I was, and even showing my brother what I had accomplished. I really started having fun when I started learning php and jquery,

What started out as a curiosity turned into me typing away at my keyboard hours on end, night would come, bedtime would pass and I wouldn't even notice. But a problem I've kind of always had is that I start many interesting projects, do 40-60% of the work and then lose interest. Thus leaving countless scrapped projects.

I didn't have access to a decent internet connection, untill very recently. I learned most of my stuff online using 15-20KBps 2G mobile internet. I would have a cap of 100mb per week, 1GB per month, etc.
This made me evolve into a offline dwelling creature with no regular backups. When the HDD on my pal of all these years, the beast Pentium 4 machine with 80Gb hdd crashed 16-17 months ago, I realized that I had no backup what so ever of anything, and basically lost every funny pic I downloaded, every line of code I ever wrote and every song I had collected over the years, foreber. I had to basically start from zero again.

One more hurdle: I had to kill off my coding interests from grade 10 through 12 because of  the way our education system is. Everything, the future of your life basically depends on these 4 grades and you have to kill anything else but study from your mind.
Which I tried to do but still didn't succeed in becoming one of the BUET,CUET,...IAN'S.
Yeah, in that sense, I am a failure.

Now I'm getting started again,
I'm learning more low level languages, starting with C. Brushing up my rusted Web Development skills. Trying to stay focused, and waiting to see where that takes me. :)
